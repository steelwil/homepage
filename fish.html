How I started this damn hobby

My dad bought a 3-foot tank. He soon lost interest and I felt sorry for the fish. I soon realized that this is not as easy as it looks, why could I not reproduce what I saw it the books? Why don’t these books tell me anything?

What is the problem?

Well the problem is that most books are quite bad they teach you stuff like what to buy and they list lots of fish, but they don’t teach the basics.

There are 3 general approaches that seem to work as far as planted aquariums go.

  1. High tech, high cost approach. See Dupla for more details.
  2. The Phosphate limited approach which I would call medium tech. See PMDD for more details.
  3. The low tech, natural aquarium approach. See Natural Aquariums site also Dan Q's guides.

Other tips

  • Under gravel filters are useless. They are good if you don’t have plants. (like in a goldfish tank)
  • Carbon dioxide is extremely important for photosynthesis. To conserve CO2 don’t use a filter that agitates the water surface. Don’t use an air stone (except maybe at night)
  • Carbon filter media is unnecessary. Use it only to remove medicines.
  • Most commercial plastic hoods a disgrace to the hobby. Buy an expensive wooden one or build your own.
  • Be very careful buying fish, most fish get too big, are aggressive or eat plants or dig!
  • Don't trust the pet shop assistant.
  • Buy as products from the super market as they are much cheaper.

Chemistry

  • With regular water changes chemistry normally takes care of itself.
  • Two chemicals Ammonia and Nitrite are deadly to fish, they are produced by fish waist, rotting leaves etc.
  • If the pH is greater than 7 Ammonia is produced.
  • If your water is saturated with Nitrate, Nitrite starts to build up.
  • Plants love Ammonia but can also use Nitrate
  • The 3 elements that must be in the water column are Calcium (Ca), Magnesium (Mg) and Potassium (K)

My setup

Dimentions: 121 * 43.5 * 38 cm, 200liters (or 47.6 * 17.1 * 15", 52.6 US Gallons)
Lighting: 2 * 40 fluorescent tubes (1 Flora-gro and 1 cool white)
Filtering: Homemade filter tank.
   
Substrate: 30mm potting soil with 30mm fine gravel on top of that
Temperature: 25ºC to 30ºC in the summer
   
Hardness: 3º KH and 6ºGH
pH: 7.5
Ammonia: undetectable
Iron: Fe+++ & Fe++ undetectable
Nitrate: 1mg/l
Phosphate: 0.1mg/l
